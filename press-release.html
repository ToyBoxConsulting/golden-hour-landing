ToyBox Consulting & Management Presents Golden Hour Unboxed: A Free Celebration of Black Entrepreneurship — and a Back-to-School Supply Drive — at Bellevue Botanical Garden

Sunday, August 23, 2026 · 6:00–9:00 PM · Free, all ages · Live performance, storytelling, the Micro Speed Pitching Showcase, and Pack-the-Box, a community school-supply drive · Part of the City of Bellevue's Cross-Cultural Center without Walls

Bellevue, WA — July 14, 2026

ToyBox Consulting & Management today announced Golden Hour Unboxed, a free, all-ages evening celebrating Black entrepreneurship in Bellevue and across the Eastside. The event takes place at the Bellevue Botanical Garden, 12001 Main St, on Sunday, August 23, 2026. Doors open at 5:30 PM; programming runs 6:00 to 9:00 PM. Admission is free.

The evening carries a back-to-school drive alongside the celebration. Pack-the-Box is a community school-supply drive held on site, collecting and distributing supplies for local students in the last stretch before the first day of class. Attendees can bring supplies, pack boxes, and take them home the same night.

The business side of the evening is the Micro Speed Pitching Showcase — a fast-format pitch stage for Black-owned Eastside businesses, in front of a live audience of their own neighbors. Around it: live performance, storytelling from Black founders, and a twilight social as the light goes down over the garden. The evening is hosted by Besa Gordon — storyteller, media personality, and founder of Besa Gordon Media. Primo's Bellevue is donating the catering labor; SYNC Seattle is the event partner.

Golden Hour Unboxed is part of the City of Bellevue's Cross-Cultural Center without Walls (CCCWW) — a pilot program within the City's Diversity Advantage Initiative, anchored in the Diversity Advantage Plan 2025–2035. It is one of twenty-seven Bellevue-based organizations producing more than seventy cross-cultural events citywide in 2026 through the program.

"Bellevue talks a lot about who is missing. This is a night where they are not missing. Black-owned businesses will be on that lawn. Kids will leave with what they need for the first day of school. It costs nothing to walk in. That is the whole event." — Katoya R. Palmer, Producer of Golden Hour Unboxed and Founder, ToyBox Consulting & Management

Golden Hour Unboxed is produced by ToyBox Consulting & Management, a Black-owned Bellevue consultancy in its 17th year, with fiscal sponsorship from Eastside for All. The event is endorsed by the NAACP, the Bellevue Chamber, and SplashForward. It is free to attend and open to all ages.

"The pitch showcase is ninety seconds and a real audience. Most of these owners have never had that. We are giving them one, in a garden, in front of their neighbors." — Katoya R. Palmer
Full run of show coming August 1. Marking the start of National Black Business Month, a second release carrying the complete run of show — performers, storytellers, partners, and the Micro Speed Pitching Showcase lineup — will be issued on August 1, 2026.

About ToyBox Consulting & Management

ToyBox Consulting & Management is a Black-owned, Bellevue-based consultancy in its 17th year, founded by Katoya R. Palmer, working at the intersection of community, equity, and execution. Katoya serves on the boards of three Bellevue civic institutions: the Bellevue Chamber of Commerce, SplashForward, and The Sophia Way. ToyBox is one of twenty-seven local organizations awarded 2026 programming funding through the City of Bellevue's Cross-Cultural Center without Walls. Golden Hour Unboxed is fiscally sponsored by Eastside for All.

About the Cross-Cultural Center without Walls

The Cross-Cultural Center without Walls (CCCWW) is a pilot program of the City of Bellevue, funded through the City's Diversity Advantage Initiative. CCCWW supports local organizations in hosting events that bring people from different backgrounds together around shared intercultural experiences. The program is anchored in the Diversity Advantage Plan 2025–2035, the City's current ten-year DEI strategic plan, and traces its origins to the City's 2014 Diversity Advantage vision: "Bellevue welcomes the world. Our diversity is our strength. We embrace the future while respecting our past."
