On equitable pay

The people who build Black spaces should not be the ones quietly subsidizing them.

This evening is built by Black professionals who agreed to take less than they are worth so the doors could open. That was a choice we made together. It is not a business model.

  • A host whose rate is $500 an hour worked the entire night for $500. She gave us a discount on a discount.
  • An event manager worth $100 an hour is being paid $40.
  • A photo and video team is donating their work outright.
  • The producer is taking $5,000 for a year of work — and that is still a discount.

So we are telling you the number instead of hiding it. $8,650 is what it costs to pay this team closer to what they are actually worth. Every dollar past the event itself goes there, and we will publish what we paid.

Recognition

A wall, not a podium.

Everyone who gives and wants to be named goes on the same wall, in the same size, in the order they arrived. We do not sort our neighbors by what they could afford. The person who covered the sound permit and the company that covered the night stand next to each other, because on August 23 they did the same thing: they made it happen.

And anyone can stay off the wall entirely. Say the word and you will not appear anywhere.

Two ways in

Find yours.

There are two ways to put money into this, and they are not the same. Pick the one that fits what you need back.

i — Direct

You get the recognition.

You support ToyBox directly. The money arrives whole and it arrives when we need it, so it can pay for anything on the list above. Your name goes on the wall. This is not a charitable contribution and there is no charitable receipt.

Support directly →
ii — Charitable

You get the receipt.

Your gift goes to Eastside for All, our fiscal sponsor. They receive the gift, they receipt it for charitable purposes, and they disburse it as we document what we spend. This route asks that you take nothing back — no logo, no listing, no mention from the stage. That is what allows the gift to be acknowledged as charitable. It is the quiet way in, by design.

Ten percent stays with Eastside for All. That is not overhead skimmed off your donation. Eastside for All organizes for a safe, welcoming and just East King County, led by and for Black and brown community members. They are the ones receiving, receipting, and safeguarding your gift. You are funding two things: this event, and them.
