On equitable pay

The people who build Black spaces should not be the ones quietly subsidizing them.

This evening is built by Black professionals who agreed to take far less than they are worth so the doors could open, free, to everyone. Some are working for a fraction of their rate. Some are donating their craft outright. That was a choice we made together, and we made it gladly. But a choice to absorb a loss is still a loss.

That loss comes to $8,650 — the gap between what this team was paid and what their work is worth. That is what your support repays. Every dollar past the event itself goes there.

Recognition

A wall, not a podium.

Everyone who gives and wants to be named goes on the same wall, in the same size, in the order they arrived. We do not sort our neighbors by what they could afford. The person who covered the sound permit and the company that covered the night stand next to each other, because on August 23 they did the same thing: they made it happen.

And anyone can stay off the wall entirely. Say the word and you will not appear anywhere.

ii — Give & get a receipt

A person who wants to give and receive a tax receipt.

Give through Eastside for All, our fiscal sponsor, and receive a charitable receipt for your taxes. You still choose whether to be listed with our supporters or to keep your gift private. This is the simplest way for a person to give.

Ten percent stays with Eastside for All. That is not overhead skimmed off your donation. Eastside for All organizes for a safe, welcoming and just East King County, led by and for Black and brown community members. They receive, receipt, and safeguard your gift — so you are funding two things: this event, and them.
