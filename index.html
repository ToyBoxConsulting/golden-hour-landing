About the Evening

When the sun softens, we show up.

August is National Black Business Month. When the sun begins its descent over the Eastside on August 23, something remarkable happens. The light softens. The air warms. People slow down — and they show up for one another.

Golden Hour Unboxed is a free, cross-cultural evening at Bellevue Botanical Garden where Black entrepreneurs, artists, families, and allies gather under the sunset to mingle, share, and rise together. It's a celebration of the many ways our community contributes to the Eastside — through art, food, storytelling, mentorship, and the everyday brilliance of building something from nothing.

This is more than an event. It's a platform — for emerging Black entrepreneurs to mingle with seasoned professionals. For families to experience cross-cultural exchange in real time. For artists, vendors, and community builders to be seen and supported.

What to Expect

An evening, five movements.

Programming designed for cross-generational connection — performances, ideas, and warmth that move from afternoon light into twilight.

i.

Sunset Sounds

Acoustic music, spoken word, and movement from cross-cultural artists — programmed for the garden's evening rhythm.

ii.

Storytelling

Personal narratives of building, belonging, and rising — voices from across the Eastside.

iii.

Micro Speed Pitching

Emerging entrepreneurs and artists share ideas shaped by their identities. Three minutes. One spotlight.

iv.

Pack-the-Box Drive

A back-to-school giving initiative powered by an upcoming nonprofit partnership. Bring supplies, leave with new friends.

v.

Twilight Social

As the sky turns lavender and gold, we gather for an unhurried social that closes the evening with warmth.

Why This Event

Bellevue is one of Washington's most diverse cities. Intentional spaces for Black entrepreneurs to be celebrated and seen by their neighbors are still rare on the Eastside.

Golden Hour Unboxed is a free, public evening built to close that gap — for Black entrepreneurs, their families, and the whole Eastside community that gathers around them.

We hold this gathering in August, during National Black Business Month, because the timing matters. We hold it at Bellevue Botanical Garden because the place matters. We hold it as part of the City of Bellevue's Cross-Cultural Center without Walls because the structure of belonging matters.

This isn't a passive cultural showcase. It's an active exchange — Black entrepreneurs, allies, and neighbors sharing names, ideas, and how to find each other after the sun sets.
